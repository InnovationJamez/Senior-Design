Saltyness of Ocean Water

The concentration of salt in seawater in about 35 parts per throusand, 35 grams per liter or 3.5%[2]. When converted to milligrams per liter the number becomes 35,000 mg per liter. This number is far beyond the safe limit for drinking, agriculture, or livestock. The safe level of salinity for irrigation is 360 mg/L and the safe level for livestock is shown in the cahrt bellow[3].The goal of this electrodialysis solution is to find the parameters that give both eanough water for a population and enough of the salt removed to be used for the three uses mentioned above.

"When constructing an ED plant for brackish water desalination, the composition of the feed is generally fixed and stack dimensions are given by the manufacturer. To achieve a certain product water quality for a given plant capacity, the required total membrane area and the total current must be determined. The required total membrane area is a function of the current density which is determined by the applied voltage and limited by the so-called limiting current density which again is a function of the stack geometry and the feed flow velocity. All the different process parameters are interrelated and must be considered when designing and optimizing an ED plant."[4]

"e.g., increasing the flow rate in a given stack design will decrease the residence time of the solutions in the stack and thus reduce the amount of salt ions removed. On the other hand, an increased flow rate allows for a higher current density, which leads to an increase of the ion removal."[4]
